TIME: Sunday, 11 PM ET

BREAKING: CoinDesk has published a leaked balance sheet from Alameda Research, the trading firm founded by FTX CEO Sam Bankman-Fried. According to the document, Alameda's $14.6 billion in assets is dominated by $5.8 billion in FTT — the native token of the FTX exchange — plus another $3.4 billion in illiquid altcoins (SOL, SRM, MAPS). Cash reserves: under $134 million. The piece, by Ian Allison, lands at a moment of extreme nervousness in crypto after a year of Luna, Celsius, 3AC, and Voyager collapses.

Within hours, Binance CEO Changpeng "CZ" Zhao posts on X: "As part of Binance's exit from FTX equity last year, Binance received roughly $2.1 billion equivalent in cash (BUSD and FTT). Due to recent revelations that have come to light, we have decided to liquidate any remaining FTT on our books." Binance has held a substantial FTT position since exiting FTX equity in mid-2021. The market reads the post as the start of a coordinated unwind.

FTT trades at $22 at the time of CZ's post — down from $25 earlier in the day. Within 30 minutes it's at $18. SBF has not yet posted publicly. Caroline Ellison, CEO of Alameda, posts: "A few notes on the balance sheet info that has been circulating recently: that specific accounting reflects a subset of our corporate entities, we have $10b+ of assets that aren't reflected there." She offers to buy CZ's FTT "block" at $22. The market views this as a defense; FTT briefly stabilizes, then resumes falling.

BTC is trading at $20,500 — already down 65% from its $69,000 peak a year ago. The market has been grinding sideways for months. ETH at $1,540. The DeFi ecosystem (Aave, Compound, MakerDAO, Curve) holds significant exposure to FTT-collateralized loans and to FTX as a venue. Solana, the L1 most associated with the SBF empire, is trading at $32 — one of its weakest levels in years. SOL holders include large institutional positions and retail bag-holders from the 2021 cycle.

Affected actors include: FTX retail users with funds on the exchange (estimated 1+ million accounts); institutional FTX customers (hedge funds, market makers, DAOs treasury-managing on FTX); Alameda counterparties (lenders who took FTT or SOL as collateral); Solana ecosystem builders and validators; DeFi protocols with large FTT/SOL exposure; crypto-native journalists and on-chain analysts (whose tweets move markets in minutes); rival exchange CEOs with positions on either side; regulators watching for the next domino; macro traders pricing crypto's correlation with risk assets and BTC.

The crypto Twitter/X commentariat is tracking on-chain wallet outflows in real time. Reports of withdrawal delays at FTX have started circulating but are unconfirmed. The next 24-72 hours will determine whether: (a) FTX honors withdrawals, restores confidence, and FTT recovers; (b) a full Lehman-style bank run hits FTX, forcing a halt of withdrawals and a cascade; (c) BTC and ETH break to new yearly lows as contagion spreads; (d) Solana suffers a separate ecosystem cratering as SBF entities are forced to dump SOL; (e) regulators step in; (f) some combination, with the speed and depth of the cascade depending on how the Twitter narrative, on-chain data, and CEX response all interact in the coming hours.

Sentiment is sharply divided: some calling this "the most overblown FUD of the year" (echoing SBF's Q3 statements that FTX is fine); others calling it "Lehman 2008 in crypto." The truth is opaque — most participants don't know the real state of FTX/Alameda books, and inference must be drawn from public balance sheets, on-chain flows, and statements by figures with conflicts of interest.
