TIME: Tuesday, 9:30 AM ET

BREAKING: The Trump administration has dissolved the National Science Board (NSB), the bipartisan oversight body that has guided NSF research priorities for over four decades. The decision was announced via a White House press release citing "strategic realignment" with national security and economic competitiveness goals.

NSF currently funds approximately 35,000 research grants per year totaling $7.8B in taxpayer dollars. The NSB had historically reviewed strategic initiatives, with 73% of FY2018–2022 strategic programs going through NSB review. Its dissolution removes the firewall between political appointees and peer-reviewed grant decisions.

Reactions are split sharply. Administration officials and aligned commentators frame the move as an overdue correction to "ideological capture" and "bureaucratic bloat." The research community — university administrators, principal investigators, first-generation researchers, and tech founders whose companies were seeded by NSF grants — is calling it "intellectual treason" and "competitive suicide." Citations of China's National Natural Science Foundation, which has maintained equivalent oversight structures for decades, appear in multiple threads.

Affected actors: NSF leadership, NSB former members, research universities, principal investigators, first-gen researchers, tech founders with NSF-seed origins (e.g. Duolingo), Senator-level political voices, federal grant administrators, science journalists, and the broader Twitter/X commentariat.
